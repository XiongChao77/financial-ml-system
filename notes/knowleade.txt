Recommended Quantitative Finance & Algorithmic Trading Books
Title	Why It’s Recommended	Main Focus
Inside the Black Box (Rishi K. Narang)	Considered a “bible” for global quantitative investors.	Explains the four pillars of quantitative investing in an accessible way: alpha models, risk models, execution, and portfolio construction.
Quantitative Trading: How to Build Your Own Algorithmic Trading Business — Ernie Chan	One of the best practical introductions.	Provides many statistically driven trading strategies and is especially suitable for your current stage of feature engineering and signal construction.
Statistical Arbitrage — Andrew Pole	A strong intermediate-level choice.	Teaches how to exploit market inefficiencies using statistics, especially useful for multi-factor models and pair trading.
Advances in Financial Machine Learning — Marcos López de Prado	Essential modern AI/ML quant reading.	Covers key financial ML problems such as non-stationarity, cross-validation failure, purging, and feature engineering. Extremely relevant to your current normalization and feature pipeline work.
📌 Classical Textbooks (Theory + Foundations)

These books form the theoretical backbone of quantitative finance and are especially useful for building foundations in mathematics, probability, statistics, and market modeling.

📘 1) Algorithmic Trading and DMA — Barry Johnson

Focus:

Trading strategies
Execution
Market microstructure

Not just about strategies — it also explains how markets actually work.

👉 Excellent for understanding real market mechanics and implementation.

📘 2) Quantitative Trading: How to Build Your Own Algorithmic Trading Business — Ernie Chan

A beginner-friendly but highly practical introduction.

Covers:

strategy construction
risk management
backtesting
alpha generation ideas

👉 Great for going from 0 → 1.

📘 3) Algorithmic and High-Frequency Trading — Álvaro Cartea et al.

A market microstructure and HFT-oriented perspective.

👉 Even if you do not plan to do HFT, this book explains why concepts such as order flow and book imbalance matter.

📘 4) Advances in Financial Machine Learning — Marcos López de Prado

A highly practical ML → alpha framework.

Topics include:

Purging
Embargo
Meta-labeling
Fractional differencing

👉 Especially valuable for someone already doing feature engineering.

📘 5) Machine Learning for Asset Managers — Marcos López de Prado

Focuses on ML methodology for quantitative investing.

👉 Helps answer the question:

“How do you actually integrate machine learning into trading strategies?”

📌 Mathematics / Statistics / Time Series Foundations

These are essential for improving your modeling, feature engineering, normalization, and forecasting capabilities.

📕 Time Series Analysis — James D. Hamilton

A classic textbook on non-stationary time series analysis.

Very useful for:

signal construction
backtesting
stationarity problems
📕 The Elements of Statistical Learning — Hastie / Tibshirani / Friedman

Often considered a “machine learning bible.”

Many financial problems become much clearer when viewed through an ML/statistical framework.

📕 Forecasting: Principles and Practice — Hyndman & Athanasopoulos (Free)

Written from an R perspective, but conceptually very clear.

Covers:

forecasting
stationarity
predictive modeling
📌 Practical Quant Strategy Books (Strategy + Lifecycle)
📙 Hands-On Machine Learning for Algorithmic Trading — Stefan Jansen

Highly recommended if you already have a Python/data background.

Covers the full pipeline:

data cleaning
feature engineering
backtesting
portfolio construction

Includes examples involving:

S&P 500
crypto
macro signals
NLP-based signals

👉 Very practical and implementation-focused.

📙 Building Winning Algorithmic Trading Systems — Kevin Davey

Focuses on the full lifecycle from alpha generation to live trading.

👉 Particularly useful for understanding:

slippage
latency
fees
execution issues
📌 Market Microstructure & Market Behavior

Understanding market microstructure is extremely important for real trading.

📗 Market Microstructure Theory — Maureen O’Hara

A more theoretical treatment of market microstructure.

📗 Trading and Exchanges — Larry Harris

Very readable and intuitive.

Explains trading mechanisms through real-world examples.

📗 The Microstructure of Financial Markets — Frank de Jong & Barbara Rindi

Helps explain:

why the order book contains information
the meaning of bid/ask spreads
how liquidity drives execution
📌 Quant + Machine Learning (Advanced)
📘 Machine Trading — Ernest P. Chan

Practical ML + quantitative strategy implementation.

📘 Advances in Financial Machine Learning (again, strongly recommended)

Especially valuable once you already have some modeling and strategy experience.

📘 Applied Quantitative Finance — Wolfgang Karl Härdle et al.

Uses real financial scenarios to explain mathematics and statistics.

📌 Crypto-Specific Reading

Crypto markets differ structurally from traditional markets.

📙 Cryptoassets — Chris Burniske & Jack Tatar

Helps deepen your understanding of crypto as an asset class and macro ecosystem.

📘 Mastering Bitcoin / Mastering Ethereum

Mostly technical books, but understanding blockchain mechanics and on-chain behavior can significantly improve your intuition for crypto alpha generation.

On-chain data remains underutilized by many traders.

📌 Backtesting Frameworks (Very Important)

The documentation and source code themselves are excellent learning materials.

Recommended frameworks:

vectorbt → especially suitable for crypto and high-frequency data
backtrader → strong for traditional rule-based strategies
fastquant → useful for rapid experimentation
📌 A Practical Learning Roadmap
🧩 Stage 1 — Foundations (2–4 weeks)

Read:

Quantitative Trading (Chan)
Trading and Exchanges (Harris)

🎯 Goal:

Understand:

market structure
trading strategy lifecycle
execution realities
🧠 Stage 2 — Data & Signal Construction (4–8 weeks)

Read:

Hands-On ML for Algorithmic Trading (Jansen)

Practice:

cleaning OHLCV/order-book data
building RSI/MACD/custom signals
basic backtesting
🚀 Stage 3 — Statistics & Machine Learning (8–12 weeks)

Read:

The Elements of Statistical Learning
Advances in Financial Machine Learning

Practice:

time-series cross validation
purge/embargo
meta-labeling
regime detection
🛠 Stage 4 — Live Trading & Risk Management (Long-term)

Read:

Algorithmic Trading and DMA
Building Winning Algorithmic Trading Systems

Practice:

slippage modeling
transaction cost analysis
risk management
portfolio sizing
